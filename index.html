Follow the following diet chart

  • Green leafy vegetables like spinach are a rich source of nitrate. Our body converts nitrates into nitric oxide. Thus, consuming green vegetables ensures blood flow throughout the body’s vital organs. Moreover, spinach controls blood pressure and dilates the arteries. As a result, it increases oxygen levels in the blood..
  • Beetroots are an excellent source of fibre. In addition, they contain folate or vitamin B9, manganese, potassium, iron, and vitamin C. Being rich in iron and folate; beetroots develop nitrates in the body. They improve oxygen absorption in the blood. Thus, they help dilate blood vessels, increasing oxygenated blood flow to tissues.
  • Carrots are rich in vitamins A, C, B3, B5, B6 and choline, potassium, iron, zinc and copper. They also contain phytochemicals that contain anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. They are essential for detoxifying the body and increasing oxygen supply to the tissues. You can consume carrots in a salad or make a glass of juice from chopped carrots
